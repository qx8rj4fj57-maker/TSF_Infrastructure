Infrastructure Build

The 10 evaluation criteria collapse into five build priorities — each with a 2026 target.

The Shared Prerequisite
Every path requires the same foundation: governance, administration, documented results, and market thesis. Build the infrastructure first. Then all three paths are simultaneously available.
The Relationship Reality
Relationships with brands exist at the program level. The infrastructure formalizes the organization around those relationships — so when a conversation gets serious, there is a real entity, real documentation, and a real operational team behind it.
Path A
DIRECT
TO EYBL
The fastest route. The hardest to control. All variables must converge simultaneously.
WINDOW: Late 2026 → 2027–28 EYBL season
Full infrastructure + EYBL-level 17U talent + established visibility at Nike-affiliated events + open market territory. Programs that get in this way have typically been building toward it for several seasons without making it the explicit stated goal.
  • All 5 infrastructure pillars complete
  • Elite 17U talent — ranked, D1-committed or bound
  • Multi-age depth — 15U and 16U with event records
  • Visibility at Nike EYBL / Scholastic or adjacent events
  • Market territory with no existing Nike conflict
Key Requirement
The program must already be known at Nike grassroots events before the conversation happens. Being known before the process begins is as important as the process itself.
Path B
SECONDARY
CIRCUIT →
EYBL
Can be the most viable route for programs that eventually reach EYBL. Builds the competitive record that significantly strengthens any subsequent EYBL conversation.
WINDOW: 2027 secondary circuit → 2028–29 EYBL conversation
Infrastructure + sponsored status with a legitimate national circuit (UAA, Adidas 3SSB, Puma CYB, New Balance Nationals) + 1–2 seasons of documented performance. Each circuit places programs in front of college coaches and builds the organizational muscle required to compete for EYBL.
  • Pillars 1–4 complete before brand commitment
  • 16U + 17U competitive with verifiable results
  • Relationship with target brand's grassroots team
  • Geographic fit with target brand's circuit coverage
  • Commitment to 1–2 season proving-ground period
Why Path B Works
Secondary circuits are not consolation prizes. They are the proving ground where programs build the operational experience and institutional credibility that makes an EYBL conversation viable — with documentation to back it up.

Less common but historically documented. The longest path — and potentially the most durable.
WINDOW: 2027–28 top-tier → 2028–29+ EYBL transition
Full infrastructure + top-tier Adidas, Under Armour, or New Balance sponsorship + sustained multi-season performance + available at a moment when Nike has a market opening the program can credibly fill. Not a controlled process — but a documented one.
  • Full infrastructure in place before any brand commitment
  • Achieve top-tier status — sustained, not one-season
  • Maintain documented performance across 2+ seasons
  • Territory remains a Nike opportunity over time
  • Maintain Nike community relationships independently
The Underlying Logic
Nike evaluates organizational credibility, market opportunity, talent pipeline, and brand fit. A program that has demonstrated all four under another brand — at the highest level — has made a compelling case.
